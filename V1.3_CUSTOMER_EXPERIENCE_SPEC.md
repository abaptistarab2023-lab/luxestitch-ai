# V1.3 Customer Experience Specification

Status: functional specification — approved direction pending, **nothing in this document has been implemented**
Inputs: [PILOT_FINDINGS_v1.2.0.md](PILOT_FINDINGS_v1.2.0.md), [HOMEPAGE_MESSAGING_REDESIGN.md](HOMEPAGE_MESSAGING_REDESIGN.md), [PRODUCT_ROADMAP.md](PRODUCT_ROADMAP.md), [BUSINESS_REVIEW.md](BUSINESS_REVIEW.md)
**No code has been written or changed to produce this document.**

## Why this isn't just a feature list

Every prior roadmap pass (`PRODUCT_ROADMAP.md`'s v1.2 Phase 2 & 3) scoped v1.3-and-beyond as additive features on top of the existing creation flow — messaging, a gallery, owned photography, bolted onto a form that was already built. The first customer pilot changed that. Three of its four findings didn't ask for a new feature next to the form — they said the form itself is wrong: customers don't want to start from blank text fields, they want to browse, see, and choose. That's not a features list, it's a different shape for the whole flow. This document specifies that new shape.

The fourth pilot finding (no admin email notification) is a Blocker and, per [USER_PILOT_PLAN_v1.2.0.md](USER_PILOT_PLAN_v1.2.0.md) §8, is being fixed as a v1.2.x patch — not held for v1.3. It's specified in full here anyway (§13) because its architecture needs to be designed once, correctly, for all four notification events — not redesigned again when v1.3 adds three more of them.

```
                                              │                                          │

                                                                                          │

                                                                        │                                    │
                                                          Email: Quote approved → business    Email: Quote declined → business
                                                                        │
                                                                In production → Completed
```

**The one architectural decision this journey requires and previous versions didn't**: browsing and personalizing (catalog through Review & Preview) no longer require an account. Registration is asked for at the moment it's actually needed — saving or submitting — not before a visitor has any reason to trust the site enough to hand over an email and password. This directly answers Pilot Finding 003 (customers expect to browse before committing) more completely than adding a browse link ever could, since today's flow still forces registration before `/dashboard/new` is reachable at all.

This is flagged as a decision, not a fait accompli, because it's a real architecture change (an unauthenticated, client-held "builder state" that only gets persisted to the database on save/submit) — see §19, Dependencies. If this is not approved for v1.3, the fallback is: keep registration required up front, and treat all of §2's new steps as happening inside the existing `/dashboard/new`, authenticated as today. Everything else in this document works either way; only the entry point changes.

## 2. New project creation flow

Replaces today's single-scroll form (`src/components/projects/ProjectForm.tsx`) with a step-by-step builder. Steps in order, each with its own wireframe in §17:

| Step | Name | Required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Entry point | — | Arrives from Catalog, Gallery, pasted URL, or "start blank" |
| 1 | Product & category | Required unless arriving with a product already chosen | Confirms item type/category if not already implied by entry point |
| 2 | Monogram / text | Required | What gets stitched — kept as today, but now step 1 of personalization, not buried mid-form |
| 3 | Font picker | Required | Visual, §6 |
| 4 | Thread color picker | Required | Visual, §7 |
| 5 | Placement selector | Required | New, §8 |
| 6 | Inspiration photo | Optional | Kept from today, §10 |
| 7 | Recipient / occasion / notes | Optional | Kept from today, lightly reordered to come after the visual choices, not before |
| 8 | Review & Preview | — | §9, always shown before save/submit |

A visible step indicator (1 of 8, with labels) persists across the flow — critical on mobile (§14), where there's no scroll-based sense of "how much is left" the way today's single long form at least implies.

## 3. Product catalog

The existing `catalog_products` table (title, `item_type`, description, `image_url`, `source_url`, `display_order`, `is_active`, `business_line`) is kept as-is and extended additively — no destructive migration, matching the pattern already established in `0008`/`0009`:

- **New `category` column** on `catalog_products`: finer-grained than `item_type` (which stays as the broader grouping used elsewhere in the schema — projects, admin filters). `category` is what the customer actually browses by (§4).
- **New `price_range_cents_low` / `price_range_cents_high`** (nullable): closes the "no pricing signal" gap `BUSINESS_REVIEW.md` and `HOMEPAGE_MESSAGING_REDESIGN.md` both flagged, shown as "From $X" on catalog cards.

Catalog browsing stays public (no login required), consistent with today's RLS policy (`"Anyone can view active catalog products"`) and with §1's guest-browsing goal.

## 5. Product gallery

Distinct from the catalog, and previously designed but not built in the original v1.2 plan (Phase 3) — reused here rather than redesigned:

- **Catalog** = inventory: blank products a customer can order and personalize.
- **Gallery** = inspiration: finished, *already-personalized* pieces (a monogrammed robe, a stitched baby blanket) showing a real font/thread/placement combination in context.

`gallery_items` table (as originally scoped): `id`, `project_id` (nullable — a gallery item may or may not trace back to a real customer project, with permission), `title`, `image_url` (LuxeStitch's own photography, not hotlinked — closing the licensing gap `BUSINESS_REVIEW.md` flagged for the catalog), `category`, `occasion`, `display_order`, `is_active`. Filterable by category and occasion. Every gallery item links into the Project Builder pre-filled with that item's font/thread/placement choice — "start something like this" — directly answering Pilot Finding 003 and the storytelling section proposed in `HOMEPAGE_MESSAGING_REDESIGN.md`.

## 6. Visual embroidery font picker

Replaces the free-text "Font Style" input (`ProjectForm.tsx:271-272`) that Pilot Finding 001 identified as the problem.

- **New `embroidery_fonts` reference table**: `id`, `name`, `preview_image_url`, `display_order`, `is_active` — a small, fixed, business-curated set (matching Finding 001's recommendation), not an open-ended list.
- **Preview approach**: real stitched-sample photography or high-fidelity renders per font, **not** a live webfont rendering of the customer's typed text. Embroidery fonts don't look like their closest web-font equivalent once stitched — showing an inaccurate live preview would be worse than the current free-text field, not better. A live-text preview using web-approximated fonts is a possible later enhancement, explicitly not required for v1.3.
- **Selection UI**: a scrollable grid of font cards (image + name), single-select, with the selected card visually distinguished by more than color alone (§15).

## 7. Visual thread color picker

Replaces the free-text "Thread Color" input (`ProjectForm.tsx:278-279`), per Pilot Finding 002.

- **New `thread_colors` reference table**: `id`, `name`, `hex_or_swatch_image_url`, `display_order`, `is_active` — again a small, fixed, real-inventory set, not an open color picker (an embroidery shop stitches with the thread colors it actually stocks, not sixteen million hex values).
- **Selection UI**: a swatch grid, each swatch labeled with its real name (e.g., "Blush Pink," not just a color chip) — both for the fulfillment-consistency reason Finding 002 raised, and because color name is required for accessibility (§15), not optional polish.

## 8. Embroidery placement selector

New concept — no equivalent exists in the app today. Where on the item the personalization goes.

- **New `placement` field** on the project (structured, small fixed set per category — a robe's placements aren't a towel's).
- **UI**: a simple diagram of the item (illustration, not a photo) with 2–4 selectable zones highlighted (e.g., robe: left chest, cuff; towel: corner, center). Each zone is a real selectable control with a text label, not just a clickable image region — a screen-reader user needs "Left chest" as an option, not a coordinate.
- Placement options and diagrams are per-category content the business needs to supply (§19) — this isn't something engineering can invent accurately without real product knowledge of what's physically embroiderable where.

## 9. Product preview before submission

A review screen, not a photo-realistic rendered mockup. Photo-realistic compositing (showing the actual monogram text rendered onto a photo of the actual product in the actual chosen thread color) is a real, valuable feature — and explicitly **out of scope for v1.3**, since it requires image-compositing tooling this spec doesn't design. Setting that expectation now avoids the review screen being read as broken when it's structural by design.

What the Review & Preview screen shows instead: the chosen product image (from catalog/gallery/URL-import/upload), the font selection (its preview image), the thread color (swatch + name), the placement (diagram with the chosen zone marked), the monogram text as typed, and all optional details (recipient, occasion, notes) — one screen, scannable, with an "Edit" link back to each step. This is the last screen before Save Draft or Submit for Quote.

## 10. Optional inspiration upload

Unchanged in substance from today's `ImageUploadStep` component — single image, uploaded to the existing `inspiration-images` Storage bucket, RLS-scoped as it is today. Repositioned as step 6 of the new flow (after the visual personalization choices, not competing with them for attention at the top of the form). Multiple images per project remains explicitly deferred to v2.0, per `PRODUCT_ROADMAP.md` — not revisited here.

## 11. Optional product URL

Unchanged in substance from today's `UrlImportStep` / Firecrawl-import flow. Repositioned as one of several entry points (§1, step 0) alongside Catalog and Gallery selection, rather than the flow's primary or only starting point as it functions today.

## 12. Quote request flow

Unchanged from v1.2.0's shipped behavior — this is the part the pilot validated as working. Submit for Quote still collects/confirms contact info and moves the project to `submitted`. The only change is what precedes it: today's flow goes straight from a blank form to this step; v1.3's flow goes through the full builder (§2) and Review screen (§9) first. The submit route itself (`src/app/api/projects/[id]/submit/route.ts`) gains one new side effect: firing the "New quote request" email (§13) — the fix already specified for Pilot Finding 004.

## 13. Email notification flow

Extends the single-event architecture already proposed for Finding 004 to all four events a customer or business needs to know about, designed once rather than four times:

| Event | Recipient | Fired from | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| New quote request | Business owner | `POST /api/projects/[id]/submit` (after the `projects` update succeeds) | Project title, category, customer name/phone, link to `/admin/projects/[id]` |
| Quote sent | Customer | `PATCH /api/admin/projects/[id]` (the `status === "quote_sent"` branch) | Amount, timeline, note, link to the project's page |
| Quote approved | Business owner | `POST /api/projects/[id]/quote-decision` (`decision === "accepted"` branch) | Project title, customer name, link to `/admin/projects/[id]` |
| Quote declined | Business owner | `POST /api/projects/[id]/quote-decision` (`decision === "declined"` branch) | Project title, customer name, link to `/admin/projects/[id]` |

```
Route handler (submit / admin PATCH / quote-decision)
        │  after the database write succeeds
        ▼
src/lib/email/client.ts   ("use server-only", same pattern as the
        │                   existing Firecrawl client)
        ▼
Email provider (Resend recommended — see Finding 004's investigation)
        │
        ▼
Recipient inbox
```

Sending is fire-and-forget relative to the request/response cycle — a failed email send is logged, never blocks or fails the underlying database action (a customer's submission must succeed even if the notification email doesn't). Both the admin-facing and customer-facing events reuse the same `client.ts` module and a shared `sendNotification(type, payload)` interface, not four separate ad-hoc implementations.

## 14. Mobile-first UX

Every wireframe in §17 is drawn at mobile width first — this isn't a "then adapt for mobile" pass at the end.

- **Wizard, not scroll**: the step-by-step structure in §2 is itself the mobile strategy — one focused screen per step beats one long scrollable form on a small viewport, which is exactly what today's `ProjectForm.tsx` is.
- **Sticky bottom action bar**: "Back / Continue" pinned to the bottom of the viewport on every step, thumb-reachable, consistent with how the existing `Button` component is already used full-width on mobile elsewhere in the app.
- **Picker grids**: font and thread-color pickers show as a horizontally scrollable row on mobile, a multi-column grid at `sm:`/`lg:` breakpoints — the same responsive pattern already established in `GiftCategoryGrid.tsx` (`grid-cols-2 sm:grid-cols-3 lg:grid-cols-4`), extended rather than reinvented.
- **Catalog/Gallery grids**: 2-column on mobile, matching the existing dashboard project-card grid pattern.
- **Placement diagrams**: sized to remain legible and tappable at mobile width — a 2–4 zone diagram, not a dozen tiny hit targets.

## 15. Accessibility

- **Color is never the only signal.** Every thread-color swatch and font-preview card carries a visible text label — not decorative, load-bearing for anyone who can't distinguish the swatches by color alone. This is the one place in this spec where accessibility and the core feature request are the same requirement, not a bolt-on.
- **Selected state uses more than color.** A chosen font/thread/placement gets a border, checkmark, or icon in addition to any color highlight, so the selection is visible under `prefers-contrast` and to colorblind users.
- **Every picker is keyboard-operable.** Tab order through grid items, `Enter`/`Space` to select — no picker that only works with a mouse or touch.
- **Wizard step changes are announced.** An `aria-live` region announces "Step 3 of 8: Thread Color" on navigation, so a screen-reader user isn't lost in a flow with no page reloads.
- **Placement diagrams have a text-equivalent.** Each selectable zone is a real labeled control ("Left chest"), never an image-only click target.
- **Contrast is checked programmatically, not eyeballed** — the same standard already applied and documented in `FINAL_REVIEW.md` for the primary button — extended to every new colored UI element this spec introduces (selected-state borders, step-indicator states).

## 16. Future multilingual support (EN/ES/PT)

Distinct from the demographic-framing critique in `HOMEPAGE_MESSAGING_REDESIGN.md` — this isn't about profiling a South Florida audience, it's about serving real language needs in a market where Spanish and Portuguese are everyday languages, not edge cases.

**v1.3 scope: architecture only, English-only ship.** Externalize every customer-facing string (UI labels, the new wizard's step names, category names, email templates from §13) into a translation-ready format now, while these screens are being built for the first time — retrofitting i18n onto already-shipped components is real, avoidable rework. Route structure: locale-prefixed paths (`/es/...`, `/pt/...`, English unprefixed as default) is the recommended approach, consistent with Next.js App Router's native locale-routing support.

**Explicitly deferred past v1.3**: the actual Spanish and Portuguese translations. `HOMEPAGE_MESSAGING_REDESIGN.md` established a specific emotional brand voice ("stitched with love," not corporate SaaS language) — translating that correctly needs a native-speaking translator reviewing for tone, not machine translation, which is real content work on its own timeline, not an engineering task that fits inside a v1.3 build cycle.

## 19. Dependencies

Real assets and decisions this spec cannot proceed without, none of which are engineering tasks:

1. **Font preview imagery** — real stitched-sample photos or renders for every font in the picker (§6). Cannot be built with placeholder images and shipped as-is.
2. **Thread color swatch data** — the business's actual thread inventory, names and accurate swatches (§7).
3. **Placement diagrams and valid zones per category** — illustration work plus real knowledge of what's physically embroiderable where (§8); engineering cannot infer this correctly.
4. **Category taxonomy sign-off** — the exact category list and naming in §4 needs business confirmation, not just this document's proposal.
5. **Approval of the guest-browsing architecture change** (§1) — the single biggest structural decision in this spec; the whole document works with either answer, but Phase C (§18) can't start without one.
6. **Email provider account** (§13) — Resend or equivalent, with a verified sending domain, per the investigation behind Finding 004.
7. **A real starting price or price range** per category (§3) — already flagged as a dependency in `HOMEPAGE_MESSAGING_REDESIGN.md`; needed again here for catalog cards.
8. **Real catalog/gallery photography** — already flagged in `HOMEPAGE_MESSAGING_REDESIGN.md` and `PRODUCT_ROADMAP.md`; this spec's Catalog and Gallery sections (§3, §5) both assume it exists.
9. **Translator resources** for EN/ES/PT (§16) — deferred past v1.3, but worth lining up early given translation timelines are typically longer than a single release cycle.

## 20. Success metrics

Tied directly to the pilot findings this spec exists to answer — the second pilot (recommended below) should measure these against the first pilot's baseline, not just declare success from usage numbers alone:

- **Browse-first adoption**: % of new projects started via Catalog or Gallery vs. a blank/URL start. A meaningful shift toward browse-first directly validates the Finding 003 fix.
- **Font/thread step completion rate**: % of customers who complete steps 3–4 without abandoning, and time-on-step compared to the old free-text fields' (unmeasured, but pilot-observed as a friction point) baseline.
- **Placement selector comprehension**: in a follow-up pilot session, % of participants who correctly explain their chosen placement back to the observer without prompting (directly testable the same way §5 of `USER_PILOT_PLAN_v1.2.0.md` tested quote comprehension).
- **Admin notification latency**: time between a customer's submission and the business owner opening/acting on it — should drop sharply once Finding 004's fix ships, independent of everything else in this document.
- **Mobile completion parity**: completion rate on mobile vs. desktop should be roughly equal, not meaningfully worse — the specific claim §14 makes.
